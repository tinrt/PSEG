Weighted Social Negative Index (WSNI): Measures the proportion of negative social sentiment relative to total interactions, scaled by 100. A higher WSNI indicates more negative interactions. Positive WSNI (up to 60) suggests dominance of negative interactions; negative WSNI (down to -40) indicates positive and neutral interactions are more prevalent; a score near zero shows a balance.

Crime Index: The total number of reported crimes per capita, multiplied by 10,000.

Social Negative Articles Ratio: The ratio of negative social articles in a specific area to the total number of such articles.
